01 — The Problem

Your patients haven't left.
Nobody followed up with them.

Most clinics focus all their energy on getting new patients. Meanwhile, thousands of patients they already treated — who already trust them — have quietly drifted away. Nobody noticed. Nobody reached out.

Problem 01
Your recall list is never fully worked
Your receptionist is managing check-in, check-out, phone calls, and rescheduling every day. There is no capacity for systematic outreach to patients who haven't been in for a year or more. They drift away silently — not because they switched dentists, but because nobody called.
Reception is fully occupied. The schedule is not.
Problem 02
This is also a patient care problem
These are your patients. You treated them. You know their history. If 18 months pass and nobody has reached out, they are not getting the preventive care they should. Untreated gum disease. Caries progressing undetected. A patient who needed a follow-up and never got one.
These patients trust you. They are just waiting to be asked back.
Problem 03
Manual outreach doesn't scale — and feels like spam
When clinics do try to work the recall list, they either send a generic broadcast SMS that gets ignored, or they ask the receptionist to make manual calls — which never happen consistently. The problem is not motivation. It is systems. You need a system that runs without your team, personalises every message, and manages every reply.
Generic SMS blasts get ignored. Personalised conversations get replies.

A dormant patient is not a lost patient.
They are your most valuable one.

When a patient goes quiet for a year or two, it is easy to write them off. But these are not strangers. They already trust you, they know your clinic, and after 18 months without a checkup — they have more to fix. Their first appointment back is worth significantly more than a new patient walking in off the street.

New patient off the street
✕ No history with you — you start from zero
✕ Typically comes in for a discounted exam & cleaning

✕ No reason to stay loyal — they can leave after one visit
